WELCOME TO TRAVNIK!

If you thought, of the moment, that this small town will not have something special to offer you

GUESS WHAT: YOU'RE WRONG!

Let's start our tour

  1. First place you need to see is the mosque in the center of the city. Entering the mosque will give you the feeling that you are back in some good old time. The sense of peace and spirit of Islam. There are shops below the mosque and the Hendek stream flows through the courtyard of the mosque.

COLORFUL MOSQUE

The colorful mosque or Suleimani was called the "Colorful mosque" because of its unusual art decorations on the interior and exterior walls. Symbol of our city!

  1. Then, the place that will provide a good view of the whole town and introduce you with its history.

THE FORTRESS OF THE OLD TOWN

This national monument of Bosnia and Herzegovina, with various traces of architectural stages from the medieval, Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian times, is a rare and valuable example of a fort in our area. On the natural elevation above the town of Travnik, surrounded by watercourses, is located the fortress of the Old town, which was built in times of danger from the invasion of the Ottoman army. In 1999, the process of reconstruction and revitalization of the fortress was initiated, and today it is the center of cultural and entertainment life of Travnik. The citizens of Travnik have long been accustomed to hosting a variety of traditional events, painting colonies, multi-colored permanent or occasional exhibitions to various events, and are very happy to respond in large numbers.

  1. One of the biggest attraction, perfect place to relax:

BLUE WATER

"Blue Water is a national monument in Travnik, Bosnia and Herzegovina. It consists of the cemeteries on Derventa with the turbot of the Travnik mufti Mehmed efendi and his wife Aisha and the Lutvina (Rudolf) kahve facility." (Wikipedia)

  1. Travnik is the only town in the world with two clock towers.

CLOCK TOWERS

These two beautiful clock towers were built in the 18th century to tick off time and help with orientation.

  1. Three oldest religious buildings in Travnik:

OLDEST RELIGIOUS BUILDINGS

  • Photo 1: Jeni Mosque, the oldest mosque in Travnik (16th century). Listed in the National Monuments of Bosnia and Herzegovina.
  • Photo 2: Catholic Church (16th century).
  • Photo 3: Orthodox Church (1854). Known for its 17th century icons.
  1. And yes, this is the hometown of Nobel Prize winner Ivo Andric. Come see his home!

MUSEUM "HOUSE OF NOBEL PRIZE WINNER IVO ANDRIĆ"

The birthplace of the only Nobel Prize winner for literature in the former Yugoslavia, since 1974, has housed a Memorial Museum consisting of a birth room, a room dedicated to the novel "The Travnik Chronicle", a photo library with photographs from the Nobel Prize, a library with an art collection and "classrooms". Behind our Nobel Prize winner is an opus of 17 books, which have been translated into 43 languages, and we are also reminded of Ivo Andrić's works: "On the Drina River," "The Damned Avlija", "The Tale of the Zizir Elephant", "The Bridge on Zepa" ...
